Obsidian's plugin directory has 2,750+ entries and a review queue stretching past 2,100 submissions with 5-month wait times. Discovery is broken. Curation is overdue.

The 24K Vault is the answer: a clean, opinionated, actively maintained pass through the entire Obsidian ecosystem — only what earns its place.

## Featured This Month

> ★ **June 2026** — [**Calendar Bases**](https://github.com/edrickleong/obsidian-calendar-bases) — community plugin by [@edrickleong](https://github.com/edrickleong)

Calendar Bases adds a calendar view to Obsidian Bases: your filtered, formula-enriched notes displayed as events on a monthly or weekly grid, positioned by any date frontmatter property. The headline feature is drag-to-reschedule — drag a note to a new date and its frontmatter updates immediately, no modal, no save step. 61K downloads in its first year.

For anyone who manages an editorial calendar, project schedule, or weekly plan inside Obsidian — this is the missing spatial layer over your structured vault data.

## Quick Start

> **New to Obsidian?** Start here. These five plugins, in order, will cover 80% of what you need on day one.

1. **[Templater](https://github.com/SilentVoid13/Templater)** — automation engine for templates with variables, JS execution, and prompts.
2. **[Dataview](https://github.com/blacksmithgu/obsidian-dataview)** — query your vault like a database. Dashboards, MOCs, automated lists.
3. **[Tasks](https://github.com/obsidian-tasks-group/obsidian-tasks)** — unified task management across the whole vault.
4. **[Calendar](https://github.com/liamcain/obsidian-calendar-plugin)** — [![24K Stable 2026-04](https://img.shields.io/badge/24K_Stable-2026--04-D4AF37?style=plastic)](CONTRIBUTING.md#stable-tier-24k-verified) month-view sidebar for daily and weekly note navigation.
5. **[Obsidian Web Clipper](https://obsidian.md/clipper)** — official browser extension for capturing web content into your vault.

For AI-assisted workflows, add **[Copilot](https://github.com/logancyang/obsidian-copilot)** or **[Smart Connections](https://github.com/brianpetro/obsidian-smart-connections)** once the basics are in place.

## Workflows & Methodologies

Established frameworks that pair naturally with Obsidian.

- **Zettelkasten** — Atomic notes with bidirectional linking. Bottom-up knowledge emergence.
- **Evergreen Notes** (Andy Matuschak) — Long-lived, concept-based, densely linked notes.
- **LYT (Linking Your Thinking)** — Maps of Content (MOCs) as flexible structure.
- **GTD (Getting Things Done)** — Capture, clarify, organize, reflect, engage.
- **PARA Method** — Projects, Areas, Resources, Archive folder structure.
- **Academic PKM** — Zotero plus Dataview plus Templater for research pipelines.
- **Developer PKM** — Git plus Local REST API plus code-block templates for engineering notes.
